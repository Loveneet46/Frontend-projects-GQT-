Embeded of Locations

1.Bangalore Palace

Built in 1878, this grand palace was inspired by England’s Windsor Castle.
It was owned by the Wodeyar Dynasty of Mysore and showcases Tudor-style architecture with elegant woodwork and paintings.

2.Tipu Sultan’s Summer Palace

Constructed in 1791, this palace was the summer retreat of Tipu Sultan, the ruler of Mysore.
It features intricate teak wood carvings and Persian-style arches.

3.Lalbagh Botanical Garden

Established by Hyder Ali in the 18th century and completed by Tipu Sultan, this 240-acre garden is famous for its glass house and diverse plant species.

4.Vidhana Soudha

Built in 1956, this iconic Dravidian-style government building houses the Karnataka State Legislature.
It is one of India’s largest legislative buildings.

5.Nandi Hills

A historic hill fortress dating back to the Ganga dynasty (9th century) and later strengthened by Tipu Sultan.
It was used as a summer retreat for British officers.

6.ISKCON Temple Bangalore

Established in 1997, this large temple dedicated to Lord Krishna features modern architecture blended with traditional South Indian temple styles.

7.Cubbon Park

Established in 1870 by British engineer Richard Sankey, this lush green park covers 300 acres and is home to historical statues and the Karnataka High Court.

8.St. Mary’s Basilica

Built in 1882, this is the oldest church in Bangalore, famous for its Gothic-style architecture and the grand feast of St. Mary held every September.

9.Ulsoor Lake

One of the oldest lakes in Bangalore, it was constructed by Kempegowda II in the 16th century and is known for its scenic beauty and boating activities.

10.HAL Aerospace Museum

Established in 2001, this museum showcases India’s aviation history, including fighter jets, helicopters, and aircraft engines developed by Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL).
